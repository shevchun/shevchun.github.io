Baccarat is one of the most popular casino games anywhere in the world. Popular among the French nobility since the 19th century, this game draws high rollers from all around the world. It is a card game played between the “player” and “banker”. The dealer deals the cards while punters place bets on the hand that is closest to 9.

As the aim of the game is based on luck, baccarat games are thought to be a game of chance. Each round of baccarat has three potential outcomes:

  • Player wins
  • Banker wins
  • Tie
 

Baccarat Styles

There are several baccarat variations available that can add more twists to the game. They include:

  • Punto Banco – The most popular version of baccarat played using up to 8 decks of cards.
  • Casino Provision – Played in private rooms for more privacy.
  • Midi and Mini Punto – Simpler versions with lower bets and faster gameplay.
  • Chemin de Fer – Claimed to be the original version where banker deals the cards.
  • EZ baccarat – A variation with a higher payout to the banker and side bets available.
 

How to Play

The basic rules for baccarat are fairly simple:

  • To begin, place your bet on either the banker’s or player’s hand. Bets are placed before the cards are dealt.
  • Once the cards are dealt, the total points for both sets are determined. Tens and face cards are not worth any points while other cards are worth their face value. Aces are worth one point.
  • To win, place your bet on the hand closest to 9 points.
  • In cases where the point total is 8 or 9, it is considered a natural win and no additional cards will be drawn.
  • In cases where there is no natural win, a third card may be drawn. The rules for the third card can be confusing. The following chart may help newcomers understand better:
 
Player Third Card Banker Draws Banker Stays
8 0 - 2 3 - 7
6 or 7 0 - 6 7
4 or 5 0 - 5 6 - 7
2 or 3 0 - 4 5 - 7
9 or 10 0 - 3 4 - 7
