The Divine Feminine Within:

The Hindu tradition refers to a woman’s genitalia as the yoni. The Daoist tradition uses other terms for female genitalia, such as the golden lotus and the gate of paradise.

As you can see, both traditions revere the female sexual organs. This perspective is quite different from that of the Western world. We can learn a great deal from these ancient traditions.

 

The Sacred Energy of the Yoni

For most women, this perspective is positive and necessary. A woman’s sexual organs are powerful, and, indeed, sacred. They deserve our respect, but unfortunately, are often abused by modern society.

The word “yoni” conjures up reverence, unlike some of the more derogatory, modern terms used to describe the female sexual organs. We should all practice this kind of sacred regard for female sexuality. It is a much more awe-inspiring way to experience sexuality and life itself.

The yoni is also sometimes called the pindika. In some yogic belief systems, the pindika is symbolic of Shakti, the Hindu goddess of feminine energy. Her male counterpart is Shiva, the Hindu god of masculine energy. Together, they represent the divine feminine and masculine energies that are said to reside within us all.

 

Where Is the Yoni Mentioned?

The yoni is included in several Hindu traditions:

  • Kashmir Shaivism, a major Hindu tradition
  • Kaula, an esoteric, Hindu practice of spiritual connectedness
  • Tantra, the Hindu art of sacred sexuality
  • The Rig Veda, a sacred Hindu text
  

According to scholars, the word “yoni” first appears in the Rig Veda. The Rig Veda is a Vedic text, which birthed the yoga tradition.

The yoni can be a spiritual metaphor for life, namely regeneration, rebirth, and the creative powers of the divine feminine. It is also symbolic of Mother Nature and her four distinct seasons, in which there is death and rebirth.

Women can become empowered when they get in touch with, and give attention to, their yoni. For this reason, the tradition known as tantra is becoming more widespread. It is time for the divine feminine to rise up!
