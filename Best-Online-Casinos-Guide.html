Gambling Legislations

Besides the statutes and licensing schemes, there are three intersecting legal frameworks concerning the gambling legislation in Malaysia. They are the Betting Act 1953, Common Gaming houses Act 1953, and the Shariah law.

The Common Gaming Houses Act 1953 states that individuals are not allowed to run or visit land-based casinos unless licensed while the Betting Act of 1953 calls for penalties up to RM 200,000 and five years in jail for anyone caught operating a betting house or patronizing one.
