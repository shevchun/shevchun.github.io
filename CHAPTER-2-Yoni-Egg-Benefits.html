What Are Pelvic Floor Muscles?

Pelvic floor muscles refer to a group of muscles often known as your “core”. These muscles work together with the back muscles, diaphragm, and abdominal muscles.

The pelvic floor stretches like a hammock from the pubic bone to the tailbone (coccyx). Important structures such as the vagina, anus, and urethra pass through the pelvic floor muscles.

 

Pelvic Floor Muscle Functions

The pelvic floor muscles are very important. Some of the functions include:

  • Support
    It provides support to the pelvic organs such as the bladder, bowel, and uterus.

  • Sexual function
    In men, the pelvic floor muscles are important for ejaculation and erectile function. In women, it contributes to arousal and sexual sensation.

  • Continence
    The pelvic floor muscles enable you to control urination and defecation. Contraction of the pelvic floor muscles tightens the openings. This allows you to delay urination and defecation until it is convenient. Relaxation of these muscles allows the passage of urine and feces.

  • Pregnancy and Childbirth
    During pregnancy, the pelvic floor muscles provide additional support for the baby. When it is time to deliver the child, the pelvic floor muscles have to be relaxed for the birthing process.
